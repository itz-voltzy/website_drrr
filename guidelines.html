Guidelines

What to do during an earthquake

SeismoSafe is designed to promote earthquake awareness, preparedness, and risk reduction. The platform aims to provide reliable, research-based, and practical guidance to help individuals, families, and school communities respond effectively before, during, and after an earthquake.

Before, During, and After

Here we have a guide for you, to follow and help. We also have a video available that can help you and be ready!

Before

    1. Make an Emergency Plan
    Know your evacuation routes.

    Identify safe spots (under sturdy desks, against interior walls). Assign a family or class meeting area. Memorize emergency contact numbers.

    2. Prepare an Emergency Kit Include:
  • Water (at least 3 days supply)
  • Ready-to-eat food
  • Flashlight & extra batteries
  • First aid kit
  • Whistle

    3. Secure Your Surroundings Bolt shelves and cabinets to walls. Keep heavy objects on lower shelves. Fix hanging lights and frames securely. Know how to turn off gas, water, and electricity.

    4. Practice Earthquake Drills Follow guidance similar to programs promoted by organizations like PHIVOLCS and FEMA: Practice Drop, Cover, and Hold On regularly.

During

    If You Are Indoors:
    DROP to your hands and knees. COVER your head and neck under a sturdy table or desk. HOLD ON until the shaking stops.
    Stay away from windows, glass, and heavy objects. Do NOT run outside while shaking is ongoing.


    ✅ If You Are Outdoors: Move to an open area away from: Buildings Trees Streetlights Power lines

    ✅ If You Are in School: Follow your teacher’s instructions. Stay under desks. Protect your head with your bag if needed. Wait for official evacuation orders.

    ❌ Avoid: Using elevators Standing near windows Rushing toward exits during shaking

After

    Stay alert. Aftershocks may follow.
    1. Check for Injuries Help injured persons if trained. Apply first aid if necessary. Call emergency services if needed.


    2. Evacuate Safely Follow evacuation plans. Avoid damaged buildings. Use stairs, not elevators.

    3. Check for Hazards Gas leaks (smell or hissing sound) Electrical damage Structural cracks Fire risks

    4. Stay Informed Listen to official updates. Follow instructions from authorities. If near coastal areas, watch for possible tsunami warnings.

    5. Remain Prepared Expect aftershocks. Do not re-enter unsafe structures until cleared.
